# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This repository centralizes key project management processes used by OctoAcme for consistent, high-quality delivery and continuous improvement.

## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme applies a structured, iterative, and customer-focused project management approach grounded in clear roles, defined deliverables, robust risk and communication protocols, and frequent retrospectives. 

**Lifecycle & Core Workflows:** Projects follow a five-phase lifecycle—**Initiation** (validate business needs and align stakeholders through a Project One-pager), **Planning** (break work into shippable increments with prioritized backlog and acceptance criteria), **Execution** (daily standups, weekly syncs, structured PR workflows, and CI/CD enforcement), **Release** (standardized deployments with pre-release checklists and rollback plans), and **Close & Retrospective** (capture learnings and convert them into tracked improvements). This end-to-end framework ensures predictable delivery and organizational learning.

**Roles, Responsibilities & Communication:** Three core personas drive execution—**Product Managers** (define what to build and measure outcomes), **Project Managers** (coordinate delivery, manage risks, and ensure transparency), and **Developers** (design, build, and test features)—each with clear ownership and complementary responsibilities. Communication follows a structured cadence: twice-weekly team standups, weekly PM-PdM alignment, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations. Risk escalation follows a three-level path from team triage to PM to Product Lead to Sponsor, ensuring visibility and timely mitigation.

**Quality Assurance & Execution Discipline:** Quality is embedded throughout execution via unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests, and security scanning in CI before any merge. Pull requests follow strict conventions—small PRs (≤400 lines), required issue links and acceptance criteria, automated tests and linting, and minimum one approval. Teams track velocity, monitor success metrics via dashboards, and maintain a live Risk Register updated weekly. A Definition of Done ensures consistency, while structured blocker escalation and comprehensive execution checklists keep projects on track.
